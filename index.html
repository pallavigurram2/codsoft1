Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi

1869-1948

Father Of Nation

  • Gandhi was born on October 2nd, 1869 in Porbandar, Gujarat.
  • Gandhi believed that everyone should have the same rights and opportunities, no matter what color they are or where they come from.
  • His father’s name was Karamchand Gandhi and his mother’s name was Putlibai Gandhi
  • Mahatma Gandhi did not believe in violence and believed that non-cooperation with evil is as much as doing good to others; he also believed that one should never hate anyone or anything in this world.
  • He led India’s non-violent resistance movement against British rule, using mass civil disobedience campaigns that were based on principles he called Satyagraha (or “truth force”).
  • He believed in freedom and equality for all people, regardless of their religion or race.
  • His philosophy of non-violence helped him to achieve his goal of Indian independence from the British Empire without bloodshed or violence.
  • Gandhi fought hard for what he believed in, even when it meant going against his own family members who disagreed with him on certain issues (like his views on women’s rights).
  • Gandhi was also known as ‘Bapu’ which means father in Gujarati.
  • He started Salt/Dandi March on 12th March 1930 and continued till 06th April 1930.
  • Gandhi was assassinated on January 30th, 1948 by Nathuram Godse, a Hindu nationalist who opposed Gandhi’s work with Muslims in India.

Principles Of Bapu

  • Ahimsa (Nonviolence)
  • Satya (Truth)
  • Brahmacharya (Self Discipline or Chastity)
  • Asteya (Non-Stealing)
  • Aparigraha (Non-Possession)
  • Sharirashrama (Physical labour)
  • Asvada (Control of Palate)
  • Abhaya (Fearlessness)
  • Sarva-Dharma-Samanatva (Equal Respect For All Religions)
  • Swadeshi (Duty towards the neighbour)
  • Asprishyata nivarana (Removal of Untouchability)
